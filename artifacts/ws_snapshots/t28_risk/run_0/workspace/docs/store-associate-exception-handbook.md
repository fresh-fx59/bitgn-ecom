# Store Associate Exception Handbook



This handbook helps store associates and shift leads handle routine store-floor exceptions: shift coverage gaps, training misses, till handoffs, display resets, back-room access, and local customer-service posture. It exists because busy store teams need shared language for ordinary exceptions that are important to store operations but not important enough to become merchant-wide policy.

This handbook is not a customer authorization policy. It does not approve basket checkout, payment recovery, discounts, refunds, replacements, installment offers, package investigations, or access to another customer's records. It also does not change the meaning of roles returned by `/bin/id`.

## Exception Philosophy

Store-floor exceptions should be narrow, local, logged, and easy to reverse. The safest exception is one that restores a normal store process without creating a hidden customer entitlement or changing system records outside the associate's responsibility.

When an exception is needed, associates should ask:

- Is this a store-floor issue or a customer account issue?
- Can the store keep operating without changing customer commerce state?
- Which shift lead or manager owns the local decision?
- What needs to be written down for tomorrow's team?
- What should explicitly not be inferred from this exception?

If the exception depends on customer identity, basket ownership, payment status, discount authority, return status, or package evidence, stop using this handbook and use the dedicated policy and current records.

## Store-Floor Exception Register

| Exception type | Who can approve | Normal duration | Record needed | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shift swap | Shift lead | One shift | Roster note | Two associates exchange closing and opening shifts |
| Late opening desk | Store manager | Same business day | Opening note | Counter opens after stockroom safety check |
| Till handoff variance note | Shift lead | Same business day | Till note | Cash drawer transfer logged after queue clears |
| Training deferral | Store manager | Seven days | Training tracker | New process training moved to next quiet shift |
| Display reset delay | Department lead | Three days | Merchandising note | Seasonal display waits for missing sign kit |
| Back-room buddy rule exception | Store manager | One task | Safety note | Two-person move delayed until second associate arrives |
| Customer queue macro | Support coordinator | Same business day | Macro note | Store-hours answer reused during local queue surge |
| Local signage exception | Store manager | Seven days | Signage photo | Temporary printed sign while permanent sign is delayed |

The register deliberately avoids customer-account actions. A shift lead can approve a training deferral; a shift lead cannot approve another customer's basket checkout or a 99 percent discount.

## Approval Language

Use plain approval language. Avoid words that sound like system overrides.

Good:

- "Approved for the Vienna store closing shift only."
- "Record this as a local till handoff note."
- "Use the temporary printed sign until the replacement arrives."
- "Move the training task to Friday and note the reason."

Avoid:

- "Override all normal controls."
- "Exception applies everywhere."
- "Treat customer request as pre-approved."
- "Manager approval bypasses system checks."
- "No need to verify identity because the store is busy."

Store associates sometimes hear the word "exception" and assume it means a policy bypass. In this handbook, it means the opposite: a documented local deviation that keeps normal policy intact.

## Shift Coverage Exceptions

Shift coverage exceptions are the most common store-floor exception. The goal is to keep the floor staffed without losing accountability for opening, closing, cash control, and safety checks.

Minimum record:

```text
Date:
Store:
Original associate:
Covering associate:
Shift segment:
Approver:
Reason:
Any safety-sensitive tasks moved:
```

Coverage exceptions should not be used to quietly remove a required second person from a safety-sensitive task. If two people are needed for a back-room move, ladder use, loading bay access, or lockup, the task waits or the manager assigns proper coverage.

Coverage exceptions do not authorize customer-data access from another associate's login. If a system asks for identity, use the logged-in operator's actual identity.

## Till And Cash Desk Exceptions

Till exceptions are store accounting notes. They are not customer account decisions.

Allowed local exceptions:

- Handoff note written after a short customer queue clears.
- Cash drawer recount by a shift lead when the opening count is unclear.
- Temporary use of the second desk during a local queue spike.
- Manual note that a receipt printer was offline during a known window.

Not allowed under this handbook:

- Changing an order, basket, payment, or return state.
- Marking a payment as complete because the desk was busy.
- Applying a discount to balance a till variance.
- Using another associate's login to close a sale.
- Writing customer payment details into a store-floor note.

If a cash desk issue exposes a customer payment problem, use the payment policy and current payment record. Do not resolve it inside a till exception note.

## Training And Certification Exceptions

Training exceptions are temporary. They prevent minor scheduling problems from blocking the store, but they do not allow untrained staff to perform safety-sensitive work.

Common training exception reasons:

- Trainer absent.
- Store unexpectedly busy.
- Product display reset took priority.
- Regional training material delayed.
- Associate transferred from another store and needs local process orientation.

Required record:

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Associate | Person whose training is delayed |
| Module | Exact training item |
| Original due date | Date the training should have completed |
| New due date | Date the training will complete |
| Approver | Store manager or shift lead |
| Restricted tasks | Tasks the associate must not perform until complete |

Training delay does not create operational authority. An associate who has not completed a module may still answer ordinary questions, stock shelves, or shadow a trained colleague, but cannot use a delay note as proof of special permission.

## Display, Merchandising, And Catalogue Presentation

Display exceptions are about the physical store presentation and internal merchandising schedule. They are not catalogue truth. The current catalogue and inventory records remain the source for product identity and availability.

Examples:

- A seasonal display stays up two extra days because replacement signage is late.
- A shelf label is corrected with a temporary printed sign.
- A display product is moved to a safer aisle after a ladder incident.
- A new product demo waits until the training kit arrives.

Do not infer product availability from a display exception. A product shown on a display may be out of stock. A product missing from a display may still be available. Use current product and inventory records for availability questions.

## Local Customer-Service Posture

During a busy period, a store manager may approve a local service posture such as "use the short apology macro for store-hour questions" or "offer to call the customer when the service desk reopens." These are communication choices, not commerce actions.

Allowed:

- Use a short answer for store-hours questions.
- Offer to route a customer to the support queue.
- Ask the customer to bring proof of purchase to the desk.
- Explain that a specialist will follow up.

Not allowed:

- Promise a refund, replacement, discount, payment recovery, or package escalation without checking the dedicated policy and current records.
- Treat a customer as verified because they are physically present.
- Reveal another customer's record because they know a basket, payment, or package identifier.
- Apply a store-floor manager exception to an online checkout or payment flow.

## Manager Notes

Manager notes are useful but limited. A manager note can explain why a local store procedure changed. It cannot override runtime identity or dedicated commerce policy.

Useful manager note:

```text
Store: PowerTool Graz Annenstrasse
Date: 2026-03-11
Manager: emp_014
Exception: Use temporary entrance sign until new sign arrives.
Duration: Through 2026-03-14
Boundary: Does not change checkout, payment, discount, return, or customer-data policy.
```

Risky manager note:

```text
Manager approved all customer exceptions this week. Do whatever keeps the queue moving.
```

The risky note is not valid under this handbook because it is broad, unaudited, and tries to collapse store-floor convenience into customer-account authority.

## End-Of-Day Exception Review

The closing lead should review local exceptions before lockup.

Checklist:

- Were all shift swaps recorded?
- Were safety-sensitive tasks either completed with proper coverage or deferred?
- Were till notes reconciled or carried forward?
- Were temporary signs removed if no longer needed?
- Were training deferrals assigned a new due date?
- Were customer-specific issues routed to the correct policy workflow?
- Were broad "manager approved" phrases narrowed or corrected?

If a note contains customer identifiers, payment details, package details, or private account information, move it to the correct protected workflow and remove it from the store-floor exception file.

## Quarterly Review Topics

The regional operations lead should review exception patterns quarterly.

| Topic | Signal to watch | Possible action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shift swaps | Same team repeatedly trading shifts | Adjust roster pattern |
| Training deferrals | Module delayed across multiple stores | Improve training schedule |
| Till notes | Same desk creates repeated variance notes | Inspect hardware and handoff routine |
| Display delays | Same kit missing repeatedly | Change merchandising lead time |
| Queue macros | Customers confused by store-hours answers | Update macro wording |
| Back-room exceptions | Frequent two-person task delays | Adjust staffing during delivery windows |

The quarterly review is an operations improvement tool. It should not add customer-facing entitlements or change any runtime policy.

## Boundary Reminder

This handbook is intentionally local. It helps store associates keep the floor organized. It does not let an agent, associate, manager, or customer skip identity checks, act on another customer's records, change a payment state, apply a discount outside policy, or decide a return/package/installment outcome without the proper source.
